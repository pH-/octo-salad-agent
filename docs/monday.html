Monday — Pinto Bean Salad with Lime and Fresh Herbs

Original recipe ↗ · Protein: pinto beans

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked pinto beans (from 1 cup dried pinto beans, soaked overnight)
  • 1 small onion (for cooking beans)
  • 2 bay leaves (for cooking beans)
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced

Tonight's steps (<10 min)

  • The night before: Pick through 1 cup dried pinto beans, place in large bowl, cover with plenty of water and soak overnight (at least 8 hours). Refrigerate.
  • Day of: Drain and rinse soaked beans. Place in large pot and cover with 2 inches cold water. Add 1 small onion (quartered) and 2 bay leaves.
  • Bring to a boil and skim off any foam. Reduce heat, cover and simmer gently for 1 to 1.5 hours until beans are tender, stirring occasionally.
  • Drain beans and discard onion and bay leaves. Let beans cool slightly.
  • While beans cook, dice 2 tomatoes, 1 red bell pepper, 1 cucumber, and 2 celery stalks. Mince 2 cloves garlic. Chop 2 tablespoons each fresh parsley and cilantro.
  • In large bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard, 3 tablespoons lime juice, minced garlic, chopped parsley, chopped cilantro, salt and pepper.
  • Add diced vegetables to bowl and toss to combine with dressing.
  • Stir cooled beans into the salad.
  • Chill for at least 1 hour before serving to let flavors blend together.
